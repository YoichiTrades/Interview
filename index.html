Notes about the Brand

Footloose

  • Duraflex is a German footwear company
  • Annual men's footwear sales is approx 1.0 billion Euro
  • Always relied on boot market for majority of their volume
  • Competes with three other major competitors
  • The four brands represent approx. 72% of the 5 billion Euro German men's boot market
  • Boots category consists of four main sub-categories
    • Work Boots
      • Largest sub-category for Blue Collar workers
        • Wage Earners who are generally working in the manual or industrial labour
        • Often require special work clothes or protective clothing
        • Replaced approx. every 6 months
    • Casual Boots
      • Fastest growing Sub-category
      • Geared more towards white collar workers and students who purchase these boots for week-end/casual wear and light work purposes
        • Salaried employees who perform knowledge work, such as those in professional, managerial or administrative positions
    • Field and Hunting Boots
    • Winter Boots
  • Four key competitors
    • Badger
    • Duraflex
    • Steeler
    • Trekker

Competitor Profiles

Badger and Steeler

  • Both well established as work Boot companies
  • Has a long history of strong brand recognition and credibility among blue collar workers

Trekker

  • Strong player in the casual boots market but very weak player in work boots

Duraflex

  • A cross between the other competitors
  • Has a significant share in both work boots and casual boots
  • Had an even stronger position in the work boot sector
  • Sales steadily declined after focusing on the growth opportunity of Casual boots since 1996
  • Badger became a much more assertive competitor in the work boot market around the same time Duraflex shifted its emphasis
  • Badger increased its market share to 43% in just three years

Consultants' role & data collected

  • Badger introduced a new line of aggressively priced work boots in fall of 2013
  • Caused Duraflex's management to re-evaluate their position in work boots because of the strong success
  • Limited additional resources, management must decide if they should focus their efforts on competing with Badger in the work boots sector, or focus their resources on further strengthening their position with casual boots.
  • Duraflex hired a leading consulting firm to conduct research to help management in decision making on January 2014
  • Consultants needed to collect information to better understand Duraflex's competitive position
  • Developed a 20 minute quantitative telephone survey conducted among 500 randomly dialed consumers in 6 primary regions of the country
  • Also completed niternal cost and pricing analysis for Duraflex's work and casual boot line
  • Market Pricing Analysis showed Duraflex competing at the premium end of the market for both its casual and work boot lines
